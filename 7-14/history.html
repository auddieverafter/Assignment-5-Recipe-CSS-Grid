History of Basque Cheesecake

If you want to learn the history of Basque Cheesecake, you are in the right place!

Basic History of Basque Cheesecake

Basque cheesecake was created in 1990 by chef Santiago Rivera at his family's restaurant, La Viña, in San Sebastián, Spain. Rivera sought to invent a new dessert and repeatedly experimented with baking a crustless cheesecake at high heat. This resulted in its signature dark, caramelized top and molten, custardy center.

Evolution of Basque Cheesecake

Rivera's goal was to create a cheesecake that was lighter, softer, and creamier than traditional American or Spanish styles. He stripped away the classic biscuit or graham cracker crust, opted for a base of just five core ingredients (cream cheese, sugar, eggs, cream, and a touch of flour), and baked it hot and fast. Instead of collapsing, the batter puffs up like a soufflé and then sinks into a rustic, beautifully flawed shape as it cools.

Popularity of Basque Cheesecake

Originally known simply as tarta de queso de La Viña (La Viña cheesecake), locals and tourists gradually began referring to it as "Burnt" Basque Cheesecake. The dessert remained a regional secret until the late 2010s, when global travelers and food bloggers discovered it. Its highly photogenic scorched exterior and dramatic "wobble" went viral on social media platforms. By 2021, The New York Times named it a defining food trend of the year.
